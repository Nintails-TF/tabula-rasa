# Behavioural Signals of Competence

## Physical Presence and Energy

**Impact:** Medium-High
- Creates first impression baseline
- Affects all interactions
- Compounds with other signals

**Perception:**
- Deliberate movement → Self-control and awareness
- Good posture → Confidence and physical capability
- Consistent energy → Good self-management and discipline
- Functional strength → Capable of handling physical demands

**Success Angle:**
- People subconsciously associate physical capability with general competence
- Signals you have excess capacity (not operating at limit)
- Creates halo effect for other judgments
- Particularly impactful in contexts requiring physicality

**Failure Angle:**
- Rushed/frantic movement → Lack of control or overwhelm
- Poor posture → Low energy, insecurity, or carelessness
- Energy crashes → Poor self-management or health issues
- Physical incapability → Dependence on others for basic tasks

**Difficulty:** Medium
- Requires consistent training (already doing)
- Posture habits take weeks to rewire
- Energy management requires sleep/nutrition discipline
- Maintains naturally once established

## Reliability in Small Things

**Impact:** Very High
- Most observable in close relationships
- Creates compound credibility over time
- Directly affects others' daily experience

**Perception:**
- Following through without reminders → Self-management and integrity
- On-time arrival → Respect for others and personal organization
- Quick response times → Attentiveness and reliability
- Remembering details → You value the person/conversation

**Success Angle:**
- Extremely high ROI - easy to do, massive impact
- Creates trust that extends beyond the specific actions
- Makes everything else you do interpreted more favorably
- Differentiates you from most people (who are mediocre at this)
- **MOST IMPACTFUL IN SHARED LIVING SITUATIONS**

**Failure Angle:**
- Needing reminders → Requires management/parenting
- Chronic lateness → Disrespect or inability to plan
- Slow/no responses → Unreliable or not engaged
- Forgetting details → Didn't care enough to remember
- Trains others to see you as incompetent/unreliable

**Difficulty:** Low-Medium
- Technically easy (just do what you said)
- Requires systems (reminders, calendars)
- Main challenge is consistency over time
- Biggest obstacle is overcommitting

## Resource Management

**Impact:** Medium-High
- Highly visible to those who interact with your environment
- Creates ambient impression of competence
- Signals preparedness and foresight

**Perception:**
- Organized tools/equipment → Competence and respect for resources
- Things in working order → Proactive maintenance mindset
- Knowing where things are → Mental organization and efficiency
- Financial buffer → Planning ability and self-control
- Sensible spending → Good judgment and priorities

**Success Angle:**
- Reduces friction in daily life (compounds efficiency)
- Projects "has their shit together" without saying it
- Enables you to help others (have tools, can lend things)
- Financial stability removes entire category of stress signals

**Failure Angle:**
- Constant searching for items → Disorganized mind/space
- Broken things left unfixed → Can't maintain systems
- Financial stress → Poor planning or impulse control
- Wasteful spending → Bad judgment or lack of discipline

**Difficulty:** Medium
- Initial organization requires significant time investment
- Maintenance requires consistent habits
- Financial discipline requires delayed gratification
- Systems need regular review and adjustment

## Information Processing

**Impact:** Medium
- More important in intellectual/professional contexts
- Less observable in casual daily life
- Creates differentiation in conversations

**Perception:**
- Reading substantive material → Intellectual curiosity and discipline
- Coherent discussion → Clear thinking and retention
- Having frameworks/models → Deep understanding vs surface knowledge
- Clean admission of ignorance → Intellectual honesty
- Following up after learning → Genuine curiosity and reliability

**Success Angle:**
- Positions you as someone who thinks deeply
- Makes your opinions more credible
- Attracts other competent people
- Compounds over time (knowledge builds on knowledge)

**Failure Angle:**
- Shallow consumption → Intellectual laziness
- Can't explain ideas → Don't actually understand
- Bullshitting when uncertain → Intellectual insecurity
- Never following up → Didn't actually care

**Difficulty:** Medium-High
- Requires consistent time investment
- Fighting against easier entertainment options
- Need to build reading stamina
- Retention requires active processing (notes, discussion)

## Social Calibration

**Impact:** High
- Affects every social interaction
- Particularly important in professional contexts
- Hard to recover from poor calibration

**Perception:**
- Reading context appropriately → Social intelligence and awareness
- Knowing when to speak/listen → Confidence without arrogance
- Adjusting communication style → Flexibility and empathy
- Handling disagreement well → Secure confidence
- Non-defensive to pushback → Intellectual security and maturity

**Success Angle:**
- Makes people comfortable around you
- Signals high general intelligence (social complexity)
- Enables influence without force
- Creates opportunities through positive interactions

**Failure Angle:**
- Misreading context → Socially unaware or self-absorbed
- Dominating conversations → Insecure need for validation
- Inappropriate communication style → Inflexible or tone-deaf
- Making disagreement personal → Fragile ego
- Defensive reactions → Insecurity masking as confidence

**Difficulty:** High
- Requires real-time processing and adjustment
- Hard to self-assess (need external feedback)
- Cultural/contextual rules vary significantly
- Improves with exposure but requires conscious practice

## Problem-Solving Approach

**Impact:** High
- Highly visible under stress/uncertainty
- Reveals true capability vs performed competence
- Creates strong lasting impressions

**Perception:**
- Practical response to disruptions → Emotional regulation and adaptability
- Staying functional under pressure → Actual competence vs fragile performance
- Resourcefulness → Intelligence and initiative
- Knowing when to ask for help → Judgment and ego management
- Figuring things out independently → Capability and self-reliance

**Success Angle:**
- Separates you dramatically from complainers/panickers
- Shows you have actual capacity (not just good conditions performance)
- Makes you valuable in uncertain/difficult situations
- Builds reputation for reliability under pressure

**Failure Angle:**
- Complaining/panicking → Can't handle adversity
- Asking for help on solvable problems → Learned helplessness
- Freezing under pressure → Performance illusion exposed
- Never asking for help → Poor judgment or stubborn pride

**Difficulty:** Medium-High
- Requires emotional regulation under stress
- Need broad knowledge base for resourcefulness
- Judgment calls are contextual and difficult
- Performance under pressure requires exposure/practice

## Environment Maintenance

**Impact:** Medium
- Very high visibility in shared living
- Creates constant ambient signal
- Affects others' daily experience

**Perception:**
- Clean environment → Self-discipline and respect for space
- Organized systems → Mental clarity and planning
- Maintained space → Taking responsibility for surroundings
- Proactive cleaning → Consideration for others

**Success Angle:**
- Zero-friction signal (people see it constantly)
- Removes negative signal (messy = incompetent)
- Shows consideration in shared spaces
- Creates better mental state for yourself (clearer thinking)

**Failure Angle:**
- Messy space → Chaotic mind or lack of discipline
- Dirty common areas → Inconsiderate or lazy
- Disorganization → Can't manage systems
- Neglected maintenance → Can't take care of responsibilities

**Difficulty:** Low-Medium
- Technically simple (just clean)
- Requires consistent habit formation
- Main challenge is maintaining standards when tired/busy
- Easier with systems (cleaning schedule, designated places)

## Morning Routine

**Impact:** Medium
- Less directly observable to others
- Creates foundation for daily performance
- Signals discipline and self-management

**Perception:**
- Waking early → Discipline and intentionality
- Consistent routine → Self-regulation and planning
- Morning productivity → Not reactive/chaotic
- Physical readiness → Takes self-care seriously

**Success Angle:**
- Sets tone for entire day (performance baseline)
- Shows you control your time vs time controlling you
- Creates extra capacity (more usable hours)
- Compounds with consistent energy levels

**Failure Angle:**
- Waking late/inconsistently → Lack of discipline
- No routine → Reactive rather than intentional
- Rushing in morning → Poor planning or time management
- Low morning energy → Poor sleep hygiene or health

**Difficulty:** Medium
- Requires sleep discipline (early to bed)
- Initial habit formation is challenging
- Easy to slip when stressed or social schedule changes
- Maintains naturally once circadian rhythm adjusts

2. **Environment maintenance** - Difficulty: Low-Medium, Impact: Medium (High in shared space)
3. **Problem-solving approach** - Difficulty: Medium-High, Impact: High
4. **Resource management** - Difficulty: Medium, Impact: Medium-High
5. **Physical presence/energy** - Difficulty: Medium, Impact: Medium-High (Already working on this)

- Environment maintenance (establish cleaning schedule, organize tools)

**Medium-term (1-3 months):**
- Problem-solving approach (practice calm responses, build resourcefulness)
- Resource management (organize equipment, improve financial systems)
