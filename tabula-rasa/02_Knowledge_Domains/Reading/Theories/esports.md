# Esports

- **Cheongjeongsu (청정수):** "Clean/Pure Water"; refers to a complete beginner or "newbie" who hasn't been "contaminated" by the game's meta or toxic veteran habits. In a community of "stagnant" or "rotten" water, a _Cheongjeongsu_ is often treated as a rare, precious resource that veterans try to protect (or humorously "corrupt") to keep the game alive.
- **Goinmul (고인물):** "Stagnant Water"; refers to high-level veterans who have mastered a game so thoroughly that they dominate the server, making it difficult for any "fresh water" (new players) to flow in.
- **Sseogeunmul (썩은물):** "Rotten Water"; describes an elite tier of players who have stayed in a game so long their skill has turned "sour," often involving the mastery of obscure engine exploits and pixel-perfect mechanics that seem like glitches to outsiders.
- **Sekimul (석유):** "Petroleum/Oil"; refers to the "fossilized" legends of ancient games (like _Brood War_) who have been compressed by decades of play into a high-energy resource, possessing a near-supernatural intuition for a game’s every heartbeat.
- **PC Bang (PC방):** The "breeding ground" or high-tech LAN cafe where these tiers of players evolve, offering the high-end gear, cheap hourly rates, and "Ramyun-to-desk" service required for the thousands of hours of grind it takes to become "rotten."
- **Hwasik (화석)**: "The Fossil" Refers to the absolute ultimate stage of a player who has outlasted entire generations and eras of the game. Often playing since the alpha or beta, spanning decades.
- **Chimbipja (침입자):** "The Intruder." Someone who didn't grow up with the game but has such high raw talent that the "Fossils" can't stop them. Usually younger players who hit like trucks.
