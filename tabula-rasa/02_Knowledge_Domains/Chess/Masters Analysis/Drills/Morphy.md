## Drill 1: Position Recognition (5 mins)

**Set up these 3 positions. Find the key move in <10 seconds each.**

### Position A: After 6...Nf6

```
Setup: White has Bc4, Qf3. Black has Nf6, Qe7, e5 pawn.
Black's weaknesses: f7, b7
```

**Find:** Double attack move **Answer:** Qb3 **Why it works:** Attacks f7 and b7, Black can't defend both

---

### Position B: After 9...b5

```
Setup: White Bc4, Qb3, Nc3. Black played ...b5 (weakening move).
Black's queen on e7, king on e8 (uncastled).
```

**Find:** Forcing sacrifice **Answer:** Nxb5 **Why it works:** Opens c-file, after cxb5 Bxb5+ Black's king is doomed

---

### Position C: After 15...Nxd7

```
Setup: Black king on e8, knight on d7, queen on e6
White has Qb3, Rd1
```

**Find:** Queen sacrifice for mate **Answer:** Qb8+ **Why it works:** Nxb8 forced, then Rd8# (back rank mate)

---

**Daily Target:** Set up all 3, find moves in <30 seconds total

## Drill 2: Pattern Hunt in YOUR Games (10 mins)

**Open your last 5 games. Look for ONE of these:**

- [ ] **Slow developer:** Opponent's king in centre move 10+
- [ ] **Double attack:** Undefended pieces on different sides
- [ ] **Development lead:** You have 3+ pieces out, opponent has 1-2
- [ ] **Material grab:** You took pawn but gave opponent time to develop
- [ ] **Back rank:** Opponent's king on 1st/8th rank with no escape

**Action:** Screenshot ONE position, note which pattern applies

**Weekly Goal:** Find 1 position per week where Opera Game principles would help
