# Tzatziki


INGREDIENTS
- 480 milliliters full-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 English cucumber
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
- 1 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped (optional)
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tablespoons lemon juice
- 0.8 teaspoons salt
- 0.3 teaspoons black pepper

STEPS
1. Grate & drain the cucumber: Grate 1 English cucumber on the large holes of a box grater. Wrap in a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth and squeeze out as much liquid as possible — this is key to a thick dip.
2. Mix: In a bowl, combine 2 cups full-fat Greek yogurt, the drained cucumber, 2 garlic cloves, minced, 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped, 1 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped (optional), 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, 1 tablespoons lemon juice, 0.8 teaspoons salt, and 0.3 teaspoons black pepper. Stir well.
3. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let the flavours meld. Taste and adjust salt or lemon before serving.
4. Serve: Drizzle with a little extra olive oil and a pinch of dill or paprika. Serve with warm pita, veggies, or alongside grilled meats.

NOTES
**Tips for the best tzatziki:**
- Use full-fat Greek yogurt — low-fat versions make it watery and bland.
- Don't skip squeezing the cucumber; excess moisture will thin the dip.
- Make it a day ahead — it tastes even better the next day.
- Some Greek recipes use white wine vinegar instead of lemon juice for a sharper tang.

STORAGE
Keeps for 3-4 days in the fridge
