# Baba Ganoush (Oven Method)

Smoky, creamy roasted eggplant dip with tahini and lemon — oven-roasted for easy home cooking.

INGREDIENTS
• 2 large eggplants
• 3 tablespoons tahini
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 lemon, juiced
• 2 tablespoons olive oil
• 0.8 teaspoons salt
• 0.3 teaspoons ground cumin
• 0.5 teaspoons liquid smoke (optional, for extra smokiness)
• 0.3 teaspoons smoked paprika, for garnish
• 1 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

STEPS
1. Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Line a baking sheet with foil.
2. Roast the eggplants: Pierce each eggplant several times with a fork. Place on the prepared baking sheet and roast, turning once halfway through, until the skin is dark and shrivelled and the eggplant is completely collapsed and very soft.
	1. Around 45 minutes.
3. Broil for char: For extra char, switch the oven to broil for the last 5 minutes, watching closely until the skin blisters and darkens further.
4. Steam and cool: Transfer the eggplants to a bowl and cover tightly with plastic wrap. Let them steam — this makes peeling much easier.
5. Peel and drain: Peel off and discard all the skin. Place the flesh in a colander and let it drain for 5 minutes to remove excess liquid, then roughly chop.
6. Mix the dip: In a bowl, combine the eggplant with 3 tablespoons tahini, 2 garlic cloves, minced, the juice of 1 lemon, juiced, 0.8 teaspoons salt, 0.3 teaspoons ground cumin, and 0.5 teaspoons liquid smoke (optional, for extra smokiness) if using. Mash and stir to your preferred texture — chunky or smooth.
7. Taste and serve: Taste and adjust salt and lemon. Spread onto a plate, drizzle with 2 tablespoons olive oil, and garnish with 0.3 teaspoons smoked paprika, for garnish and 1 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish. Serve with warm pita or vegetables.

NOTES
**Getting smokiness from the oven:** Line your baking sheet with foil and let the eggplant get properly collapsed and dark — don't pull it too early. A small amount of liquid smoke stirred in at the end can help replicate the flame-charred flavour.

**Make ahead:** Baba ganoush keeps well in the fridge for up to 4 days. The flavour actually improves overnight.

**Optional additions:** A pinch of cayenne for heat, or a dollop of Greek yogurt for extra creaminess.

STORAGE:
- Keeps 4 days in the fridge. 
	- Taste can improve with time.
