# Lavash Flatbread

A thin, soft Armenian flatbread that's quick to make and perfect for wraps, dips, or snacking.

Serves: 4

INGREDIENTS
• 480 millilitres all-purpose flour
• 180 millilitres warm water
• 2 tablespoons olive oil
• 0.5 teaspoons salt
• 1 tablespoons sesame seeds or poppy seeds (optional)

STEPS
1. Mix the dough: In a large bowl, combine 480 millilitres all-purpose flour and 0.5 teaspoons salt. Add 2 tablespoons olive oil and 180 millilitres warm water and mix until a smooth dough forms. Knead for about 2–3 minutes until soft and pliable.
2. Rest the dough: Cover the dough with a clean towel and let it rest for 15 minutes.
3. Preheat: Preheat your oven to 230°C (450°F). Place a baking sheet or pizza stone in the oven while it heats up.
4. Roll it out: Divide the dough into 4 equal balls. On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball out as thin as possible — almost translucent, roughly 30x40 cm (12x16 in). Sprinkle with 1 tablespoons sesame seeds or poppy seeds (optional) if using and give one final roll to press them in.
5. Bake: Carefully transfer the rolled dough onto the hot baking sheet. Bake for 4–6 minutes until the edges are golden and the surface has light brown spots.
6. Cool and serve: Remove from the oven and let cool on a wire rack. It will crisp up slightly as it cools. Serve immediately or store in an airtight bag to keep it soft.

NOTES
**Soft vs. crispy:** For softer lavash (great for wraps), pull it out a little earlier and stack the pieces while warm — the steam keeps them pliable. For crispier lavash (great for dipping), bake a minute or two longer and let cool uncovered.

**No oven?** You can cook lavash in a dry cast-iron skillet over high heat for 1–2 minutes per side.
