# Egg Fried Rice

**INGREDIENTS (for 3 cups cooked rice, 2 batches):**

**Rice:** 3 cups cooked day-old rice (broken up, dry, no clumps)
**Eggs:** 4 eggs (beaten fresh right before cooking)
**Aromatics:** 2-3 scallions (whites separate from greens)
**Oil:** 3 tablespoons neutral oil total (vegetable, canola)

**Soy sauce (PRE-MIXED in small bowl):**
- 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce
- Mix together, then split in half for 2 batches

**Seasoning:**
- White pepper (couple pinches)
- Salt (add at end after tasting - start 1/4 tsp, adjust up)

---

**BATCH 1 (1.5 cups rice):**

**1. Heat wok 3 FULL MINUTES**
- Medium-high heat
- Don't rush
- Oil shimmer test when ready

**2. 1 tbsp oil + half scallion whites**
- Swirl oil
- Add whites, stir 10-15 sec until fragrant and sizzling

**3. 1.5 cups rice in**
- Break up clumps immediately
- Spread-sit-toss pattern for 2-3 min
- LOUD sizzling entire time

**4. HALF the soy mix**
- Drizzle around edges (not middle)
- Toss immediately to coat
- 30-60 sec more tossing

**5. Push rice aside, 0.5 tbsp oil in space, HALF the eggs**
- Should bubble immediately (heat test)
- Let set 5-10 sec
- Scramble in space
- Fold rice back when eggs mostly cooked but still wet

**6. Pinch white pepper, toss, TASTE**
- Add salt gradually until "oh damn" level
- Half the scallion greens
- Toss, kill heat

**7. Bowl, cover loosely**

**BATCH 2: Repeat with other half**
