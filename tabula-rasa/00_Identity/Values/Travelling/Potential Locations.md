# EU

## Romania

**Country overview**
- Tax: 10% flat income tax
- Citizenship: 8 years residence
- Language: Romanian (Romance, accessible)
- EU member, NATO member
- Excellent internet infrastructure

### Cluj-Napoca
- Population: ~400,000
- Cost of living: €1,200-1,500/month comfortable
- Rent: €400-600 nice central one-bedroom
- Tech scene: Strongest per capita in Romania, real cybersecurity presence
- Vibe: University town, young, walkable, manageable size
- Outdoor access: Carpathian foothills nearby, good hiking
- Airport: Regional, European connections, one-hop to most destinations
- Best for: Structured routine, tech networking, sustainable long-term base

### Bucharest
- Population: ~2 million
- Cost of living: €1,400-1,800/month comfortable
- Rent: €500-800 nice central one-bedroom
- Tech scene: Largest in Romania, Bitdefender headquarters, more security companies
- Vibe: Chaotic, sprawling, more happening but harder to navigate
- Outdoor access: Requires more effort to escape the city
- Airport: Major international hub, best Romanian connectivity
- Best for: Maximum professional opportunity, urban energy, don't mind chaos

# APAC

## Singapore

**Country overview**
- Tax: 15-18% effective at senior salary levels, no capital gains
- Citizenship: Rare, discretionary, no clear path
- Residency: Employment Pass requires local sponsor, PR competitive
- Language: English official, easy to navigate

### Singapore (city-state)
- Population: ~5.5 million
- Cost of living: $3,500-4,500 SGD/month comfortable ($2,600-3,300 USD)
- Rent: $2,500-4,000 SGD nice one-bedroom ($1,850-3,000 USD)
- Tech scene: Strong finance and enterprise security, regional HQs, APAC hub
- Vibe: Ultra-modern, efficient, dense, regulated, world-class food
- Outdoor access: Limited within country, regional travel compensates
- Climate: Hot and humid year-round, no seasons
- Airport: World-class hub, Asia-Pacific gateway
- Best for: Local high-paying role, APAC career building, regional exploration base, urban convenience

## Australia

**(Nice to live, but taxes and cost of living is crazy high. If environment is superb or fits well, then it's worth.)**

**Country overview**
- Tax: Progressive rates up to 45%
- Citizenship: 4 years residence (1 year as PR)
- Residency: Skilled visa points-based, employer sponsorship possible
- Language: English

### Sydney
- Population: ~5.3 million
- Cost of living: $4,000-5,000 AUD/month comfortable ($2,600-3,300 USD)
- Rent: $2,500-3,500 AUD nice one-bedroom ($1,650-2,300 USD)
- Tech scene: Largest in Australia, finance and enterprise security, growing start-up scene
- Vibe: Coastal, outdoorsy, spread out, expensive, beach culture
- Outdoor access: Beaches, Blue Mountains nearby, excellent natural environment
- Climate: Mild winters, warm summers
- Airport: Major international hub, long-haul connections
- Best for: Work-life balance priority, outdoor lifestyle, English-speaking, accepting high costs for lots of sun and vibe.

### Melbourne
- Population: ~5 million
- Cost of living: $3,500-4,500 AUD/month comfortable ($2,300-3,000 USD)
- Rent: $2,000-3,000 AUD nice one-bedroom ($1,300-2,000 USD)
- Tech scene: Strong, more start-up-oriented than Sydney
- Vibe: Cultural, coffee obsessed, arts scene, more European feel
- Outdoor access: Great Ocean Road, Yarra Valley, varied landscapes
- Climate: Variable, four seasons, cooler than Sydney
- Airport: Major hub, slightly fewer connections than Sydney
- Best for: Cultural lifestyle, slightly lower costs than Sydney.

## Malaysia

**(Remote working ONLY, not local)**

**Country overview**
- Tax: Progressive rates up to 30%, foreign income often exempt
- Citizenship: Extremely difficult, not realistic path
- Residency: MM2H program (long-term visa), employment pass, DE Rantau (digital nomad)
- Language: English widely spoken, Malay official

### Kuala Lumpur
- Population: ~2 million (metro ~8 million)
- Cost of living: RM 5,000-8,000/month comfortable ($1,100-1,750 USD)
- Rent: RM 2,000-4,000 nice one-bedroom ($440-880 USD)
- Tech scene: Growing, regional offices, less developed than Singapore
- Vibe: Modern, multicultural, sprawling, good food, relaxed pace
- Outdoor access: Batu Caves, highlands accessible, jungle nearby
- Climate: Hot and humid year-round, monsoon seasons
- Airport: Major regional hub, good APAC connectivity
- Best for: Low-cost APAC base, relaxed lifestyle, remote work friendly, chill Singapore alternative.

## Thailand

**(Remote working ONLY, not local)**

**Country overview**
- Tax: Progressive rates up to 35%, foreign income complexity
- Citizenship: Extremely difficult, not realistic path
- Residency: Various visa options including new digital nomad visa, Elite visa (paid program)
- Language: Thai local, English in tourist/business areas

### Bangkok:
- Population: ~10 million
- Cost of living: ฿50,000-80,000/month comfortable ($1,400-2,300 USD)
- Rent: ฿15,000-30,000 nice one-bedroom ($430-860 USD)
- Tech scene: Limited security market, start-up scene growing, more remote worker hub
- Vibe: Chaotic, vibrant, incredible food, hot, sensory overload
- Outdoor access: Limited from city, islands and nature require travel
- Climate: Hot year-round, rainy season
- Airport: Major regional hub, cheap flights throughout Asia
- Best for: Low-cost remote work base, food and culture experience, accepting limited career options.

### Chiang Mai

- Population: ~130,000 (metro ~1 million)
- Cost of living: ฿35,000-55,000/month comfortable ($1,000-1,600 USD)
- Rent: ฿10,000-20,000 nice one-bedroom ($290-570 USD)
- Tech scene: Minimal, pure remote worker hub
- Vibe: Laid back, digital nomad saturated, temples, cooler than Bangkok
- Outdoor access: Mountains, trekking, nature accessible
- Climate: Cooler than Bangkok, smoky season March-April
- Airport: Regional, connections through Bangkok
- Best for: Lowest cost base, nomad community, outdoor access, fully remote lifestyle. Think Iceland/Greenland but for Asia.

## China

**Nice to visit - absolutely NOT to work in.**

**Country overview**
- Tax: Progressive rates up to 45%
- Citizenship: Essentially impossible for foreigners
- Residency: Work visa requires employer sponsorship, increasingly restrictive
- Language: Mandarin 
- Work culture: 996 (9am-9pm, 6 days) normalized at tech companies, intense pressure, political considerations in workplace.

### Beijing
- Population: ~22 million
- Tech scene: Major tech hub, government and enterprise focus, Baidu headquarters
- Vibe: Political capital, historic, sprawling, harsh winters, intense air quality issues
- Outdoor access: Great Wall sections accessible, Western Hills
- Climate: Continental, freezing winters, hot summers, dust storms spring
- Airport: Massive international hub

### Shanghai
- Population: ~26 million
- Tech scene: Financial centre, international business hub, growing start-up scene
- Vibe: Most cosmopolitan Chinese city, colonial architecture, modern skyline, commercial energy
- Outdoor access: Limited from city, water towns nearby for day trips
- Climate: Humid subtropical, unpleasant summers, mild winters
- Airport: Major international hub, two airports

### Shenzhen
- Population: ~18 million
- Tech scene: Hardware capital of the world, Tencent and Huawei headquarters, start-up ecosystem
- Vibe: Young city, built in 40 years, manufacturing meets innovation, less character than Beijing/Shanghai
- Outdoor access: Beaches, Hong Kong accessible, mountains nearby
- Climate: Subtropical, humid, mild winters
- Airport: Good regional connectivity, often route through Hong Kong

### Chengdu
- Population: ~21 million
- Tech scene: Growing western hub, gaming industry presence, lower costs than coastal cities
- Vibe: Relaxed by Chinese standards, famous food scene, panda sanctuary, teahouse culture
- Outdoor access: Gateway to Sichuan mountains, excellent hiking and nature
- Climate: Humid, overcast, mild year-round
- Airport: Expanding international connections

## Russia

**Nice to visit - ABSOLUTELY 0% CHANCE TO WORK IN. No until geopolitical situation improves (hopefully sooner rather than later)**

**Country overview**
- Tax: 13% flat income tax (attractive on paper)
- Citizenship: Possible but complex, 5 years residence
- Residency: Work visa requires sponsorship, bureaucratic process
- Language: Russian 
- Work culture: Variable, can be intense, relationship-driven, hierarchical
- Current situation: Sanctions, international isolation, unpredictable legal environment for foreigners

### Moscow
- Population: ~13 million
- Tech scene: Historically strong, Yandex and Kaspersky origins, brain drain ongoing since 2022
- Vibe: Grand, imperial, intense, expensive, world-class metro, cultural depth
- Outdoor access: Limited from city, requires travel for nature
- Climate: Harsh winters, pleasant summers, long dark season
- Airport: Major hub, though connectivity severely reduced post-sanctions

### Saint Petersburg
- Population: ~5.5 million
- Tech scene: Secondary to Moscow, some presence
- Vibe: European feel, canals, white nights, architectural beauty, cultural capital
- Outdoor access: Baltic coast, Karelia accessible for wilderness
- Climate: Cold, damp, grey winters, magical summer white nights
- Airport: Reduced international connections currently
