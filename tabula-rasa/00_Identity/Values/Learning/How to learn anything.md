# How to Learn Anything

## The Core Loop:

**Exposure → Understanding → Application → Gaps → Repeat**

Every skill and domain responds to this cycle. The techniques change, the loop doesn't.

---

## The Fundamentals:

- **Time is the substrate.** There are no shortcuts, only better ways to spend the hours. Techniques improve your rate of learning, they don't eliminate the hours required.
- **Retrieval beats review.** Don't re-read. Close the book and reconstruct from memory. Every effortful recall strengthens the trace far more than passive exposure.
- **Space it out.** Review material just as you're about to forget it. Cramming produces fluency that evaporates. Spaced repetition produces retention that compounds.
- **Application reveals truth.** You don't really know something until you've tried to use it and had it break on you. Struggle and failure are the signal, not the problem.
- **Teaching is the highest test.** If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet. Teach it, write it out in plain English, explain it out loud.

---

## The Daily System

1. **Flashcards** — keep discrete knowledge retrievable (Anki)
2. **Reading + plain English notes** — build the conceptual map, tie abstractions to real examples.
3. **Hands-on practice** — operationalise the knowledge, expose gaps, put in reps, find out what is most useful.
4. **Do it in the wild** — case studies, real-world examples, adjacent material.

---

## The Honest Warning

The routine can become the goal. Doing cards and ticking boxes feels productive. Real struggle doesn't. Make sure the uncomfortable parts — genuine application, hard problems, facing gaps — never quietly get dropped from the system.

Mastery is this loop, repeated daily, across years. Simple formula. Hard execution.
