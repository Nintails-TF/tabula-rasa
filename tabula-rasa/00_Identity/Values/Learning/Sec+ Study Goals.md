# Learning
## Phase Breakdown

### 📚 Phase 1 — Foundation (March 5 – March 28 | ~24 days)

_Slower, deeper reading pace — no rushing chapters_

**Daily structure (3 hrs):**
- **1hr 45min** — Sybex reading (1 chapter/day comfortably, with time to pause and think)
- **45min** — Anki: new cards from today's chapter + due reviews
- **30min** — End-of-chapter questions + note weak areas

- **Read a section** (not the whole chapter — a subsection)
- **Close the book mentally** — what were the 2-3 things that actually matter here?
- **Make cards for those things** — focus on:
    - Definitions you couldn't fully explain in your own words
    - Anything with a number (ports, key lengths, timeframes)
    - Acronyms
    - "X is used for Y" relationships
    - Things you almost skipped because they seemed obvious ← those bite you on the exam
- **Move on** — don't make cards for everything, only what requires _retrieval practice_

This gives you breathing room to actually _understand_ concepts rather than just cover them.

---

### 🔄 Phase 2 — Review & Practice (March 29 – April 13 | ~16 days)

_Weak area focus + serious practice exam ramp-up_

**Daily structure (3 hrs):**
- **45min** — Sybex targeted re-reads (wrong answer topics only)
- **1hr** — Anki heavy review
- **1hr 15min** — Full or half practice exam + review every wrong answer in depth

You can do a full timed practice exam every 2 days here, which is where big score gains happen.

---

### 🎯 Phase 3 — Final Push (April 14 – April 20 | 7 days)

_Consolidation only, no new material_

**Daily structure (3 hrs):**
- **30min** — Domain summary skims (Sybex chapter summaries)
- **1hr** — Anki due queue, clear it daily
- **1hr 30min** — Full timed practice exam daily + targeted review

**April 20th:** Anki light review only, 1 hour max. Rest.
