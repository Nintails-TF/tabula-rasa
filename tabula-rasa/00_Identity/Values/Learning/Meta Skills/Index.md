## Pathway:

Foundation - Making everything else work:

1. Metacognition: Without accurate self-assessment, you can't direct effort effectively towards any other skill.
	1. Know oneself first, before seeking knowledge outside the self in a sense.
2. Memory: Without memory, you can't access the majority of information that you focus upon.
	1. It's been eroded by modern culture a large amount as well, so it's more of a competitive advantage too.
	2. You free RAM needed to recall things for other cognitive processes.
3. Tolerating Uncertainty and Confusion: You are setting the mental foundations in place. Any learning requires it.
	1. It makes practice able to be performed in the first place.

Core Skills - Primary engines of learning:

4. Active and Analytical Reading: Helps you extract as much juice from sources of media like Books, papers, reports, documentation. Helps because you read everyday.
	1. You can be given the same resources as other people, can gain more.
5. Asking Good Questions: This reality checks your own ideas, it can cut off weeks of learning and messing around.
	1. It makes other people around you more useful and helpful. Because you make it easier for them to explain things to you.
6. Writing: Turning your vague thoughts and theories into something concrete that you and others can understand.
	1. You learn by placing things down in a page or document where things succeed or fall flat.

Amplifiers - Boost returns on foundations/core skills:

7. Pattern Recognition: It converts the previous inputs into automatic and quick responses.
	1. Can build confidence and the appearance of ability, but you've got to be patient and careful with it.
8. Synthesis: Combining knowledge from multiple domains and seeing how things connect is important, but requires good enough inputs first.
	1. Cross-pollination is where most seriously good ideas and inventions come from.

Accelerants - Catalysts, modifies the rate of skills:

9. Speed of Iteration: Changes how fast you are cycling through an activity. You need to first extract lessons and move slowly to build up the processes well, then break things fast.
	1. Save it for last, because you want the road in place before going 150 MPH.
