# How SOC Teams Interact within Workplaces:
## Different Kinds of SOC:

### In-House/Internal SOC:

In-house SOC teams occur for organisations that have a great emphasis on cybersecurity. Often this covers fields which are either high value or heavily regulated. Though depending on leadership, risk tolerance, costs, talent, etc. orgs can outsource to MSSPs. 

Personally, any field here SHOULD have a dedicated SOC team once they are at a certain size, as MSSP isn't context sensitive enough and likely has multiple priorities or teams on-going at once, you aren't the main focus as you would be in a dedicated in-house team.

For example:

**Highly Regulated:** (*We have to have teams, or the government screws us + High Value*)
- Finance/Banking - Banks, Investment firms, payment processors.
	- Risks losing capital or getting sued/legal action to oblivion otherwise.
- Healthcare - Hospital, insurance companies, pharmaceutical firms.
	- Patient health records, medicine IP and details, juicy target.
	- Public healthcare often struggles though, compared to private.
		- Leadership often focused on keeping quality of service running and medicine aspect, not cybersecurity angle - they are stretched thin.
		- Typically also wants on-site.
- Government/Defence - Federal agencies, military, intelligence
	- Certain government fields are more protected that others. Defence and military take it seriously; your local council or small agency - likely less so.
	- Often requires vetting + citizenship which dilutes talent pool more.
- Energy/Utilities - Power grids, oil/gas, water treatment
	- National emergency if these go down, have to keep them protected.
	- Typically also wants on-site.

**High Value:** *(We have valuable data / reputations and can't risk losing it)*
- Technology/FAANG - Major tech companies 
	- High value IPs, customer data, platform security/reputation.
- Telecommunications - ISPs, mobile carries/network infra (massive attack surface)
	- Hackers would love to have access to the network capacity/machines within these systems, hence the need.
	- Also attractive for surveillance and espionage. APTs/Nation state actors target.
- E-commerce/Retail - Major retailer (customer payment data, supply chain)
	- PCI-DSS compliance, big attack vectors, large breach harms customer confidence.
- Aerospace/Defence Contractors - Boeing, Lockheed Martin, etc. 
	- It's pretty obvious why they care about cybersecurity. (Don't want missiles/tanks/gun designs leaked.)
	- ITAR compliance requirements, national security, and classified designs.

**Emerging Fields:** (*Same as high value, but building up to the level of them*)
- Cryptocurrency/Financial Tech (FinTech)
	- Extremely high value as the product requires cybersecurity to protect assets.
- Cloud Service Providers
	- Same as fintech, customers need a reliable service and product.
- Gaming/Entertainment
	- Growing industries, service abuse or piracy is also a concern here. Getting games or movies leaked, DDOS during major events/releases, Anti-cheats, competitive integrity, etc.
	- esports is also an emerging field within an emerging field.
