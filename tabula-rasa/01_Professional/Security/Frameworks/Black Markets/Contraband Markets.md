# Contraband Markets:

## Darknet Drug Markets

The largest category on the darknet by volume. Demand is high, constant, and draws from an enormous existing userbase — making it the most commercially viable illegal service online.
## Buyers

|Factor|Details|
|---|---|
|**Access**|Tor browser used to navigate .onion links and locate products|
|**Vendor Selection**|Reputation and review history are the primary trust signals|
|**Shipping**|Domestic shipping preferred to avoid customs seizure|
|**Payment**|Monero (XMR) favoured for its untraceability|
|**Security**|Escrow systems protect both parties from fraud|
|**Delivery**|PO boxes or alternate addresses used over primary residences|
## Vendors

|Factor|Details|
|---|---|
|**Setup**|Profile created with a vendor bond paid upfront to establish legitimacy|
|**Listings**|Products listed with descriptions, weights, prices, and photos|
|**Reputation**|Reviews and transaction history drive trust; higher ratings command premium prices|
|**Sourcing**|Ranges from independent dealers to connections with larger trafficking networks|
|**Payment**|Monero (XMR) held in escrow until delivery is confirmed|
|**Packaging**|Vacuum sealed and disguised within everyday items — books, clothing, food|
|**Shipping**|Standard postal services used; drop addresses over personal residences|
|**Communications**|PGP encryption standard for all buyer-vendor messaging|
|**Opsec**|Operations compartmentalised; vendors often active across multiple markets|
|**Platform Fee**|Markets take 2–4% per transaction|
## Markets

|Factor|Details|
|---|---|
|**Access**|.onion domains via Tor; some markets invite-only for added security|
|**Infrastructure**|Servers hosted anonymously across multiple jurisdictions; no single point of failure|
|**Vendor Onboarding**|Bonds required to list; creates financial stake in good behaviour|
|**Listings**|Structured categories filterable by drug type, price, location, and rating|
|**Escrow**|Funds held until buyer confirms delivery, protecting both parties|
|**Reputation System**|Ratings and reviews mirror Amazon/eBay; community accountability drives quality|
|**Revenue**|2–4% commission on transactions plus listing fees and vendor bonds|
|**Communications**|Built-in encrypted messaging with PGP as standard|
|**Disputes**|Dedicated arbitration with market admins acting as mediators|
|**Community**|Forums like Dread serve as hubs for news, reviews, and security tips|
|**Resilience**|Demand migrates instantly to competitors when markets are taken down|
|**Vulnerabilities**|Exit scams, law enforcement infiltration, and admin opsec failures|

## Alternate Goods

Far less prevalent than drugs, these markets are better understood as **Criminal-to-Criminal (C2C)** services. Catering primarily to other criminals rather than everyday users. Demand is lower, more specialised, and carries significantly higher legal risk.

---

### Weapons:

Firearms, ammunition, suppressors, and illegal modifications are traded primarily between criminals seeking untraceable hardware. Ghost guns — weapons with no serial numbers — are particularly common. Demand is niche and largely limited to those already operating within criminal networks.

---

### Counterfeit Goods:

Fake currency, forged luxury goods, counterfeit clothing, and fraudulent academic certificates. Primarily serves small-time fraudsters and scammers looking to either resell fakes or pass them off as legitimate. Lower risk than other categories but also lower profit margins.

---

### Identities:

Forged passports, national IDs, driver's licenses, Social Security cards, and utility bills. Primarily purchased by other criminals to enable fraud, money laundering, or to evade law enforcement. A core C2C service as legitimate buyers rarely have use for fake identities.

---

### Financial Details:

Stolen credit card data, cloned cards, bank account credentials, and cryptocurrency obtained through theft. Sold in bulk to fraudsters who then liquidate the value through purchases or money mule networks. One of the more automated and scalable criminal markets.

---

### Chemicals:

Precursor chemicals used in drug manufacturing are the primary listing type. Serves criminal supply chains rather than end users. Heavily monitored by law enforcement given the direct link to drug production and the occasional presence of hazardous or toxic substances.

---

### Human Trafficking:

Forged documents enabling trafficking operations are the primary darknet component. the actual trafficking itself operates offline. Deeply intertwined with organised crime networks. One of the most aggressively monitored categories by Interpol and national agencies globally.

---

### CSAM:

The most universally condemned category, reviled even within criminal communities, where it is actively banned on most markets. Operates on isolated, dedicated networks entirely separate from mainstream darknet markets. The highest priority target for global law enforcement, with agencies like the FBI, Europol, and Interpol dedicating significant resources to takedowns.
