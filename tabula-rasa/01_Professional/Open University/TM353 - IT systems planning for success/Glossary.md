# Glossary

Any bold words, enter them here:

- **Systems thinking** - A set of components interconnected for a purpose. ^st-def
	1. A system is an assembly of components connected together in an organised way.
	2. The components are affected by being in the system and are changed if they leave it.
	3. The assembly does something - carries out a task, fulfils a function.
	4. **The assembly as a whole has identified by some observer who is interested in it.**
- **Sociotechnical** - IT systems cannot be understood or designed without both considering social aspects (organisational and people) as well as technical aspects (hardware and software). ^SoTech-def
- **Evolutionary** - IT systems are flexible and fluid they incrementally evolve and change over time as organisations, people, and technical abilities change. ^evo-def
- **Technology** - Technology concerns itself with understanding how knowledge is creatively applied to organised tasks involving people and machines that meet sustainable goals. ^tech-def
- **Boundary** - What a system cannot or should not do. ^Bo-def
- **Environment** - Factors that influence a system or that a system influences that are outside of the system. ^Env-def
- **Open Systems** - A system that interact and exchange information with its environment, rather than operating in isolation. ^OS-def
- **Sub-systems** - A system within an already existing system. e.g. An MMO will have a PvP, PvE, Trading, social, guild, sub-systems. ^subSys-def
- **Hierarchy** - A structure in which people/systems/ideas are ranked according to their authority, level of importance or complexity. ^Hier-def
- **Emergence** - The idea that the whole is greater than the sum of it's parts. ^Em-def
- **Successful System** - A system that achieved what was intended, was operational at the time and cost that was planned, the project team and users are satisfied with the result and continue to be happy afterwards. ^SucSys-def
- **Stakeholder** - Any group or individual who can affect or is affect by the achievement of the organization's objectives. ^Stakeholder-def
