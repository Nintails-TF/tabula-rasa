# TM352 Glossary
## Week 9:

- **Consumerisation** - The phenomenon where technological innovation originates in consumer markets and subsequently drives adoption and change in enterprise/business contexts. This represents a reversal of the traditional innovation flow, where breakthroughs typically moved from military and big business to consumers. ^Consumerisation-def
- **Consumer Market** - The market comprising individuals and groups who purchase items and services for personal use rather than for business or resale purposes. This is distinct from enterprise or B2B markets where purchases are made for organizational use. ^Consumer-Market-def
- **Laggard** - The final 16% of a population to adopt a technological innovation. Laggards are typically suspicious of innovations and change in general. They often have limited resources and require certainty that an innovation will not fail before committing to adoption. ^Laggard-def
	- This term has become somewhat loaded over time, but it isn't meant in a demeaning or derogatory manner - since sometimes waiting is the rational choice. 
	- Price drops, bug fixes, feature refinement before committing resources.
