# Psychometric Instruments

## General Personality & Behavioural Assessments:

### Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (TKI)

Measures conflict-handling styles along two dimensions: assertiveness and cooperativeness. Identifies five modes: competing, collaborating, compromising, avoiding, and accommodating. Commonly used in conflict resolution training and negotiation workshops.

### Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)

Categorizes individuals into 16 personality types based on four dichotomies: extraversion/introversion, sensing/intuition, thinking/feeling, and judging/perceiving. Widely used in corporate settings despite criticism regarding validity and test-retest reliability.

### Big Five (OCEAN Model)

Measures five core personality traits: openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, and neuroticism. Considered the gold standard in personality research with strong empirical support.

### DiSC Assessment

Categorizes people along four dimensions: dominance, influence, steadiness, and conscientiousness. Popular in corporate training for team-building and communication workshops due to accessibility and intuitive results.

### Enneagram

Identifies nine interconnected personality types based on core motivations and fears. Popular in personal development contexts though lacking robust empirical validation.

### Clifton Strengths (formerly StrengthsFinder)

Identifies individual's top strengths from 34 talent themes. Developed by Gallup and widely used for career development and strengths-based coaching.

## Hiring & Selection Instruments

### Hogan Personality Inventory (HPI)

Measures bright-side personality characteristics relevant to workplace performance. Based on the Big Five model but designed specifically for occupational settings.

### SHL Occupational Personality Questionnaire (OPQ)

Assesses 32 personality characteristics relevant to workplace behavior. Widely used globally for selection and development.

### Predictive Index Behavioral Assessment

Measures four primary behavioral drives: dominance, extraversion, patience, and formality. Popular for hiring and team dynamics.

### Caliper Profile

Measures 21 personality traits predictive of job performance. Used for selection, development, and team building.

### Situational Judgment Tests (SJTs)

Present hypothetical workplace scenarios to assess judgment and decision-making. Harder to fake than personality inventories and can be role-specific.

## Leadership Assessments

### Hogan Development Survey (HDS)

Measures 11 derailment risks that emerge under stress: excitable, skeptical, cautious, reserved, leisurely, bold, mischievous, colorful, imaginative, diligent, and dutiful. Critical for identifying leadership blind spots.

### Motives, Values, Preferences Inventory (MVPI)

Measures core drivers and values that determine job satisfaction and cultural fit. Part of the Hogan suite.

### Leadership Circle Profile

360-degree assessment mapping creative competencies and reactive tendencies. Produces distinctive circular visualization showing leadership effectiveness patterns.

### Korn Ferry Leadership Assessments

Suite of assessments based on extensive research into executive competencies. Frequently used in succession planning and high-potential identification.

### Emotional and Social Competency Inventory (ESCI)

Based on Daniel Goleman's emotional intelligence framework. Assesses self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, and relationship management through 360 feedback.

### CCL Benchmarks 360

Center for Creative Leadership's flagship 360-degree assessment measuring leadership competencies and potential derailment factors.

### Multifactor Leadership Questionnaire (MLQ)

Measures transformational, transactional, and laissez-faire leadership styles. Based on Bass and Avolio's Full Range Leadership Model.

## 360-Degree Feedback Instruments

### Leadership Circle Profile

Combines 360 feedback with a developmental framework distinguishing creative competencies from reactive tendencies.

### CCL Benchmarks 360

Assesses 16 leadership competencies and five potential derailment factors through multi-rater feedback.

### Korn Ferry 360

Customizable 360 platform aligned with Korn Ferry's leadership competency framework.

### DDI Leadership Mirror

360 assessment focused on leadership behaviors tied to business outcomes.

---

## Emotional Intelligence Assessments

### Emotional and Social Competency Inventory (ESCI)

360-degree assessment based on Goleman's EI model. Measures 12 competencies across four clusters.

### Mayer-Salovey-Caruso Emotional Intelligence Test (MSCEIT)

Ability-based assessment measuring four branches of emotional intelligence: perceiving, using, understanding, and managing emotions. Considered more scientifically rigorous than self-report EI measures.

### EQ-i 2.0

Self-report measure of emotional intelligence across five composite scales: self-perception, self-expression, interpersonal, decision-making, and stress management.

## Team Assessments

### Belbin Team Roles

Identifies nine team roles individuals tend to adopt: plant, resource investigator, coordinator, shaper, monitor evaluator, teamworker, implementer, completer finisher, and specialist.

### Team Management Profile (TMP)

Based on Margerison-McCann Team Management Wheel. Identifies preferred work functions and team roles.

### Five Behaviors of a Cohesive Team

Based on Patrick Lencioni's model. Measures trust, conflict, commitment, accountability, and results at the team level.

## Clinical & Diagnostic Instruments (Reference Only)

### Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory (MMPI)

Clinical assessment screening for psychopathology. Not typically used in standard corporate settings but may appear in high-security or high-stakes selection contexts.

### 16PF (Sixteen Personality Factor Questionnaire)

Measures 16 primary personality traits. Has both clinical and occupational applications.
