# Partnership Principles

## 1. Communicate Openly, Clearly and Kindly

We commit to honest, transparent and respectful communication.

- We speak clearly, explain terms, and ask for clarification when needed
- We are mindful of language, tone, and how our words may land
- We create space for questions, challenge, and dialogue
- We communicate in timely, proportionate ways and avoid dominating conversations

## 2. Practise Deep Listening and Inclusive Participation

We listen to understand, not to respond or assume.

- We actively listen to what is being said, including the principles and emotions behind it
- We value silence as reflection and ensure space for quieter voices
- We avoid interrupting or speaking over others
- We recognise that confidence does not equal contribution

## 3. Value Difference, Experience and Humility

We respect different forms of knowledge, experience, and expertise.

- We are curious about different perspectives and lived experiences
- We recognise that no one is the expert on everything
- We remain open to learning and changing our views
- We challenge the assumption that there is only one 'right' way

## 4. Build Trusting, Relational and Emotionally Aware Partnerships

We see partnership as relational work, not just task delivery.

- We recognise that emotions and feelings are part of collaborative work
- We speak kindly, support one another, and build relationships over time
- We check in regularly on how the partnership is working
- We take time to reflect together and adapt when needed

## 5. Be Conscious of Power and Commit to Inclusion

We acknowledge that power is experienced differently and act intentionally to address it.

- We are aware of how roles, status, and confidence shape participation
- We work to ensure everyone has the opportunity to contribute
- We create psychological safety so questions and challenge are welcomed
- We are patient, understanding, and reflective about our own position

## 6. Share Responsibility, Accountability and Commitment

We take collective responsibility for the partnership and its impact.

- We attend, contribute, and follow through on agreed actions
- We take responsibility for our decisions and behaviour
- We face challenges and difficult issues together
- We agree clear, proportionate ways to reflect, learn, and hold each other accountable
