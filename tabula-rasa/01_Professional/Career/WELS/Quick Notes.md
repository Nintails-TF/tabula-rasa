## WELS Academic Integrity Action Plan:


The plan reframes academic integrity around an **ethics of care** and **restorative justice**, recognising that many Level 1 students are new to higher education conventions and need support rather than punishment. 

The aim is to develop students' academic practices through compassion, integration into teaching, and structural changes to assessment — not just enforcement.

**Four priorities:**

1. **Co-ordinated leadership** — faculty-wide oversight via the Associate Dean and AHOS, embedding three School Academic Integrity Leads, standardising ACO workload allocations, and moving to case allocation by qualification.
2. **Embedding good academic practices in teaching** — integrating academic integrity into every qualification through interactive activities, consistent referencing guides, AL professional development, and tuition strategies.
3. **Assessment design** — applying Academic Integrity Principles for Assessment Design and GenAI guidance, co-ordinating deadlines across modules, and using early assessments developmentally with feed-forward.
4. **Reducing referrals (focus on Level 1)** — developing care-based guidance for staff and changing how Level 1 cases are managed, including reduced use of similarity software at Level 1.

## Institutional EDI Plan:

The Open University wants the Athena Swan Silver award for the whole organisation. They have succeeded in getting women into STEM, but now are looking at gender equality in a more broad sense, looking at where men are underrepresented and supporting trans individuals.

EDI = Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion.

Priorities are:

**Objective 1 - Leaders demonstrate a visible commitment to inclusive practices**
1. Leadership: Have to do EDI training as part of CDSA (Career Development Staff Appraisal). 
	1. Do diversity training as part of performance review/promotions.
2. Staff also need to do EDI training as well in accordance to leadership too, and leaders need to enforce this on staff.
3. Leaders handle complaints with discriminatory matters.
4. Leaders will do mentoring opportunities
5. Leaders ensure digital and physical access is inclusive.

**Objective 2 — Leaders will drive diversity initiatives and address underrepresented groups in their teams**

1. Each unit has an EDI representative (EDI rep / EDI Champion) with protected time allocated to oversee projects and interventions linked to EDI KPIs.
2. EDI representation at Leadership Team meetings is mandatory, and EDI is a standing agenda item.
    1. Leaders ensure the EDI rep is actively included, not just present.
	    1. This tells me, that leadership has had EDI reps, but they were somewhat tokenistic and didn't do much in the past.
3. Leaders monitor and track their unit's demographic data for both staff and students.
    1. Use the data to identify gaps and trends, not just report them.
4. Leaders are accountable for addressing under-representation in their unit — specifically gender, ethnicity, and declared disability.

**Objective 3 — Leaders will ensure an equitable experience for staff and students**
1. Leaders take responsibility for equity in staff experience, including remuneration and rewards.
2. Leaders ensure equitable access to development opportunities and career progression for all staff.
    1. Check who is being put forward for stretch projects, promotions, and training — not just who asks.
3. Leaders have oversight of equitable student outcomes.
    1. Engage with a diverse range of students as part of this process, not just aggregate data.
