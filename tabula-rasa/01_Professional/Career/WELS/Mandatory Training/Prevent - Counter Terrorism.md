# Counter Terrorism and Security Act (2015):

The counter terrorism act of 2015 mandates that Prevent and CONTEST should happen within educational systems. The prevent duty involves stopping people being drawn into terrorism.

These include but aren't limited to:

1. Tackling the causes of terrorism
2. Intervening early and supporting people vulnerable to radicalisation
3. Enable people who have engaged in terrorism to disengage and rehabilitate.

Due regard involves:

> *Place an appropriate amount of weight on the need to prevent people from becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism when they consider all the other factors relevant to how they carry out their usual functions*

# Contest (2023):

The government strategy to counter terrorism is built around the 4 P's

1. Pursue - Stop terrorist attacks
2. Protect - Strengthen defences against terrorism
3. Prepare - If an attack cannot be stopped, limit the damage.
4. Prevent - Stop people from becoming terrorists or supporting extremism.

The university focuses on preventing.

# Terrorism (2000):

The terrorism act defines the following as terrorism:

- The use or threat of action (serious violence against a person, serious damage to property, endangering a person’s life, serious risk to the health and safety of the public, serious interference/disruption of an electronic system).
- Designed to influence the government or to intimidate the public or a section of the public.
- Made for the purpose of advancing a political, religious, racial or ideological cause.

# Basic Requirements:

RHEBs (Relevant Higher Education Bodies) with more than 250 students have to do the following:

- Leadership - Meets 4 times per year with the Prevent Steering Group and partners.
- Prevent lead - All orgs need a prevent lead. In the case of the OU, this is the prevent coordinator ([Prevent-Coordinator@open.ac.uk](mailto:Prevent-Coordinator@open.ac.uk))
- Risk Assessment - Risk assessments show how likely members are to be driven into terrorism.
- Prevent action plan - how can we mitigate risks and what policies and procedures need updated.
- Training
- Policy and compliance - OU policy is reviewed by the Prevent Steering Group.

RHEBs also need to balance counter-terrorism with free-speech. If someone is controversial and you cancel them, more people are drawn to them with the Streisand's effect, if you give them a platform fully it can also be bad. You want to allow free speech but put in rail guards.
