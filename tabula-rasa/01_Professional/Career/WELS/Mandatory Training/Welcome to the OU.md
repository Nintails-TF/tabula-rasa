# Our Story:

The OU setup in 1969, and over the last 57 years has delivered education in a distanced format. Opening up learning to all people, places, methods, and ideas.

The charter that was written in 1969 enabled a university in the air. And technology has enhanced this further.

## How the OU is Led:

The OU governance and leadership is structured like so:

- The **Council** is the main governing body, responsible for the University’s overall direction, finances and people.
	- These are a group of trusted experts that are the formal leadership/board members.
- The **Senate** is responsible for teaching, learning and academic standards.
	- These set the academic standards for the OU.
- The **Vice-Chancellor (VC)** leads the University day-to-day, supported by the **Vice-Chancellor's Executive (VCE)** team.
- The **Chancellor** and **Pro-Chancellor** have key leadership roles set out in the University’s Charter.
- **Students** also play an important role through representative groups.

# Our Strategy:

The university strategy is Learn and Live. It sets out ambitions for allowing people to thrive despite economic, political, and social change.

Vision: Life changing learning that enriches society.
Values: Inclusive, innovation, responsive.

The goals of the OU are:
- Greater reach
- Success for students
- Societal Impact
- Equity
- Environmental and Social Sustainability.

The seven enablers are:
1. Living our values
2. Supporting each other
3. Managing change well and continuously improving
4. Employing secure and effective technologies
5. Using data and evidence in decision making
6. Building on our uniqueness as a four nations university
7. Stewarding our finances.

# Who Studies with Us:

The OU student base has a wide range of diversity. They are one of the largest universities in Europe with over 200,000 students.

- 79% of directly-registered OU students work part-time during their studies.
- 21% of directly-registered OU students work full-time during their studies.
- 25% of new OU undergraduates are under 25.
- 75% OU undergraduates had no previous HE qualifications on entry.
- 30% students declaring a disability studied with us in.
- 39,000 students had one A level or a lower qualification at entry in.
- There are over 8,700 international students.

## ViA (Values in Action):

The OU behaviours and values are a key part of who is recruited, developed, and recognised.

Values in Action are:
- **Explored at interview**, particularly when applying for new roles or opportunities for career progression.
- **Discussed in annual review meetings (Career Development Staff Appraisal (CDSA))**, helping you reflect on what you’ve achieved and how you’ve demonstrated the OU values in your work.
- Used to celebrate success through reward mechanisms, including **peer to peer Shout Outs** and **Going the Extra Mile (GEM) awards.**

# OU Behaviours

## Inclusive

### Respectful
- Take time to learn how your new team works, including preferred ways of communicating and working together.
- Ask questions thoughtfully and consider colleagues' perspectives, experience and workload.
- Listen carefully in meetings and acknowledge others' ideas, even while you're still building confidence.

### Proactive
- Introduce yourself beyond your immediate team and make intentional connections with people you'll work with regularly.
- Join staff networks to build relationships, broaden your understanding of the OU community, and contribute to belonging.
- Speak to your manager early about any adjustments or support you might need — and be an ally by encouraging inclusive practice.

---

## Innovative

### Curious
- Ask "How do we do this at the OU?" and show interest in the wider context — not just the task in front of you.
- Explore training and development opportunities, such as workshops, digital learning, or role-specific programmes.
- Seek feedback early and use it as a tool for learning, not just performance.

### Collaborative
- Build relationships with key people in your role — including colleagues outside your immediate team.
- Share ideas early and check understanding before decisions are made, especially when work impacts others.
- Offer support where appropriate — small contributions help build trust and teamwork quickly.

---

## Responsive

### Flexible
- Stay open to OU processes and ways of working — they may differ from previous organisations.
- Adapt to changing priorities as you learn the role and the wider context.
- Try different ways to stay organised while getting up to speed (e.g., checklists, shadowing, or buddy support).

### Outcome Focused
- Keep students and colleagues in mind: How does this help someone learn, succeed or feel supported?
- Agree priorities with your manager early and check in regularly to stay focused on what matters most.
- Reflect on your progress and capture examples of how you're demonstrating the behaviours to support CDSA conversations.
	- Career development opportunities.

# Your Career

As part of the 5 week probation, we'll set and deliver goals and resources. The CDSA (Career Development Staff Appraisal) which is basically a performance review.

Performance conversations are important in helping members do their best work:
- Build Trust and Psychological Safety
	- Make people feel heard and valued. So they can raise concerns and be honest about what is working and isn't.
- Create Clarity and Understanding
	- Align expectations, priorities, goals, and reducing confusion and supporting people on what matters.
- Support Development and Growth.
	- They provide space to reflect, recognise progress, and identify needs.
- Strengthen Inclusion and Belonging
	- Creating a culture of recognising individual circumstances and valuing different perspectives.
- Address issues early and positively.
	- Stop problems snowballing.
- Connect individual contributions to the OU mission and strategy.
	- Good performance should be linked to how this contributes to their shared mission and strategy.

70% of development comes from experience, 20% comes from connection with others, and 10% comes from education or studies.
