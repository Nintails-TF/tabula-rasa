# Goals On The Goal:

## Discovering Goals:

- Long-term goal: Work in cybersecurity as a senior within red teaming/pen testing + cloud security. Or work as a SOC analyst long term. Remotely.
	- Steps needed: Continuous learning towards cybersecurity, building up a portfolio of work and projects, active CTF challenge participation, building networks and connections through doing, time-management skills.
	- If I achieved: I'd feel happy, but would wonder "what's next?"
	- If missed: It wouldn't be a big deal as getting my foot in the door is what matters, the exact title doesn't matter hugely as long as its hands-on-technical work.
- Ideal environment: An office or remote place, ideally not too loud - not against long commute times if required.
	- I'd prefer to work office hours, but the nature of some roles means I'd have to be on-call or working night-shifts (24/7 SOC)
	- I would be expecting a high salary once I gained experience and expertise, but even at the junior level. I'm saying around £30-40k is a good starting point. 
		- <30k offers are worth for me building my CV/Job approach/interview experience, but I'm not taking them.
- Development opportunities: I'd take whatever my employer would offer, I know that I'm in an industry where self-study is mandatory, so I'd be learning on the side as well.
- Nightmare of a job: Being paid poorly, doing boring/meaningless work. (GRC, Security Awareness Training, security as compliance and box ticking.)
	- If I ended up in nightmare role, I'd be ruthlessly developing my own skills in my spare time to pivot out of them, But to pick these roles in the first place, I'd have to be very desperate.
