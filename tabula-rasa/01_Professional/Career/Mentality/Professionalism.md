# Professionalism

**Definition:** Being competent and reliable with no drama.

## Communication:
- Respond to emails or phone calls in reasonable time.
- Being honest with lack of skills/ability.
- Giving clear updates and following through.
- Not oversharing personal information.
## Work Execution:
- Completing assigned tasks without reminders.
	- Occasionally, go above and beyond.
- Following established processes.
- Asking clarifying questions rather than guessing.
- Admitting mistakes quickly and fixing them.
- Not making your problems other peoples emergency.

## Social Dynamics:
- Pleasant but not trying to be everyone's best friend
- Helping colleagues when it's reasonable, declining when it's not.
- Staying neutral in politics
	- Not "no personality", just not making work an identity.
- Not creating gossip or drama
- Showing up to meetings on time, contributing when relevant, not dominating.
	- Engaged but not dramatic.

## Boundaries:
- Working scheduled hours, not doing overtime
- Not working late hours.
- Taking vacation days with no guilt
- Saying no to unreasonable requests calmly.
- Not making work ethic part of personality.

**When young:**

Show more fire, work a little harder, you're in that wealth acquisition phase. So its OK

- Ask more questions
- Ask for feedback
- Go above and beyond strategically.
- Admit mistakes and ask questions more often.
- Be more social, build a network and understanding of company culture.
- Stay late a little more, don't say yes all the time, but be a little more aggressive.

**When old:**

You're in maintenance mode, push back on busy work leave on time, do more meaningful work.

- Be selective with energy
- Admit mistakes and fix them quickly.
- Say no to problems that are caused by bad planning.
- Delegate or pushback on low-value tasks.
- Mentor when asked, but don't make it identify.
