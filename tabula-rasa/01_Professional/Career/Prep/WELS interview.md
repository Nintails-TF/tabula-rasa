# Answers:

## Question 1:

I'm studying 3 computing science modules and 1 business module at the moment. 

- **B329 - Leadership in a changing world:** 
	- Is about leadership strategies in a turbulent environments: AI, climate change, populism. and how anyone can lead as it's a set of skills not a fixed personality.
		- Constructive Dissent, positive sum versus zero-sum thinking, turbulence, precarity, situational, collaborative, follower-centered leadership.
		- Leadership isn't just directive and about authority or position to make decisions.
- **TM352 - Web, Mobile, and Cloud:** 
	- It's a technical module about how to build websites, making them work on mobile devices, and how cloud computing works, beyond marketing.
		- React, Svelte, AWS, Azure, OpenStack.
- **TM353 - IT systems planning for success**
	- It's all about understanding why IT projects succeed or fail. This gets achieved through careful modelling and systems design.
		- IT systems are sociotechnical - when you are making a solution you have to consider organisation and people first, then hardware and software second.
- **TM354 - Software Engineering**
	- Similar to TM353, but focussing on how to build quality software, it hones in on the technical aspects and simulates how a developer would actually work on a problem or contract. 
		- So how to meet the demands of a client, building diagrams and understanding how to deliver a solution.

## Question 2:

I've most enjoyed my leadership and my IT systems planning for success module. As they've both touched and explored the ideas in intimate detail about how do systems such as organisational leadership and software development function and factors to consider when operating within them to reach success.

The best moment was working in a group setting for the B329 - Leadership challenge. As it was a ripe environment to test what happens when leadership theory enters practice. Since there was real stakes on the line, people wanted to get a high mark for the EMA. 

We delt with lots of uncertainty - with group members being active and inactive, but I found that my best way of contributing with others was to limit the amount of uncertainty and make it easier for people to get up to speed. 

I did this, by making meeting notes and sharing them to members who couldn't make it, Suggesting topic to go with, but not forcing it upon others, taking up an editorial role and taking all the chunks of work we had an combining them into a cohesive report.
