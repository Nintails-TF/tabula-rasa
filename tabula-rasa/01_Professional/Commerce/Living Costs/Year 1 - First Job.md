# First Graduate Job — Housing Rules:

Salary: Assume £30k minimum (anything above is bonus)
Take Home Pay: £2,050/month (After taxes and NI)
Monthly Budget: £1,050/month
Bug Bounties: £167/month (2k yearly) (Beginner still learning)
Monthly Investment Target: £1,000/month+ (£1,750 would be great, but I can invest more at mid-level or next year)

## Rent:

No more than 35% of income.

| Scenario                        | Maximum    |
| ------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Rent + all utilities included   | £700/month |
| Rent only, no utilities         | £615/month |
| Absolute ceiling, no exceptions | £750/month |

### Housing Categories:
|Type|Description|Pros|Cons|Verdict|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Studio Flat**|Everything in one room or very small separate spaces. Self contained — own kitchen, bathroom, front door. Like Angela's uni flat.|Fully private, no neighbours sharing space, simple self contained living|Small square footage, can feel cramped long term, pricier than HMO|Good stepping stone. Comfortable solo option if budget allows|
|**HMO**|Own room rented individually. Shared kitchen, sometimes shared bathroom. Like Sea Braes or The Hub.|Not dependent on others paying rent, bills often included, cheapest route to £1,000 investable|Shared common areas, quality depends on housemates, cleanliness variable but manageable|**Best financial option on £30k. Start here.**|
|**Flatshare**|Practically same as HMO day to day but legally different. Everyone signs lease together. Best for friend groups only.|Social, homely feel, good with trusted friends|Joint tenancy legal risk, one person's failure affects everyone, avoid with strangers|**Avoid unless with people you completely trust**|
|**1 Bedroom Flat**|Fully self contained. Own front door, separate bedroom and living room, own kitchen and bathroom.|Maximum privacy, proper separation of living and sleeping space, genuinely feels like a home|Expensive — difficult on £30k without compromising investment target|**Ideal long term goal. Revisit when salary grows.**|
|**Maisonette**|Essentially a 1 bedroom flat but across two floors within a larger building. Own entrance.|More space, house-like feel, better separation of floors, usually slightly cheaper than equivalent house|Can be more expensive than regular flat, ground floor can have damp issues|**Good upgrade from 1 bed when ready. Worth considering.**|
|**New Build Flat**|Purpose built modern apartment. Better insulation and soundproofing than older conversions.|Cheaper to heat, better build quality, modern facilities|Smaller square footage for money, can feel sterile, potential service charges and leasehold complications|**Functional and practical. Check small print carefully before signing.**|
|**Managed Apartment / Build to Rent**|Entire blocks purpose built for renting, run by professional management company. Gyms, communal spaces, concierge.|Professional management means no rogue landlord, higher quality, amenities included, genuine community sometimes|Higher rent — likely above £700 all inclusive on £30k, slightly corporate feel|**Aspirational option. Revisit when salary is £35k+**|
HMO OR Studio are main options. Good location can be slightly more expensive if it saves on transport.
